Munnar, nestled in the Western Ghats of Kerala, India, is a breathtaking hill station known for its rolling green hills, sprawling tea plantations, and mist-covered landscapes. Situated at an altitude of around 1,600 meters above sea level, Munnar is a haven for nature lovers and adventure seekers. The town boasts a cool climate, making it a popular retreat from the heat of the plains. Visitors can explore the picturesque tea gardens, hike through scenic trails, and visit attractions like Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr. Munnar's serene environment, combined with its vibrant flora and fauna, creates a tranquil escape, earning it the title of "Kashmir of South India.
