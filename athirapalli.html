Athirappalli Waterfalls, often referred to as the "Niagara of India," is one of the most stunning natural attractions in Kerala. Located in the Thrissur district, it cascades from a height of approximately 80 feet, creating a spectacular sight amidst lush greenery and rocky terrains. The waterfall is formed by the Chalakudy River, which originates from the Sholayar ranges, making it a prominent tourist destination and a vital ecological hotspot in the region.

The beauty of Athirappalli lies not only in its thundering waters but also in the serene atmosphere it offers. The area is surrounded by dense forests, home to rich biodiversity, including endangered species like the Lion-tailed Macaque. Visitors can explore the picturesque trails leading to the base of the falls, enjoy panoramic views from vantage points, and immerse themselves in the soothing sounds of nature.
